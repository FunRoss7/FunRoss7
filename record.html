This isn’t a résumé. It’s an account of the systems I’ve worked on, the constraints they lived under, and the decisions that made them hold or fail. Most of it happened in places where reliability mattered more than elegance, and where “good enough” had to actually be good enough.

Reliability first

I build systems that have to behave. Most of my work sits in the layer between hardware, simulation, and operations — where assumptions get tested quickly and failure is expensive.

I’m drawn to problems where the system matters more than the code itself: timing, interfaces, failure modes, and how everything holds together when something inevitably goes wrong.

I don’t chase perfect designs. I try to find designs that survive contact with reality.

Selected Work
01
Flight & Simulation Systems
Built and maintained simulation environments used to test flight software against real-world conditions. The goal wasn’t visual fidelity — it was behavioral accuracy. If the simulation lied, the system failed later.
Worked across physics models, hardware interfaces, and real-time constraints to make sure what we saw in simulation matched what the vehicle would actually do.
02
Distributed Systems
Designed and supported systems operating across large, distributed networks where uptime and recoverability mattered more than theoretical efficiency.
Focused on failure handling, system visibility, and making sure problems surfaced quickly and clearly — because they always happen, and they rarely happen cleanly.
03
Tools & Infrastructure
Built internal tools to reduce friction in development and testing — automation, diagnostics, and systems that let engineers move faster without breaking things.
A lot of this work never gets seen directly, but it changes how quickly a team can learn and iterate.
How I work
Approach

I tend to start from constraints instead of abstractions. What has to be true? What fails first? What do we actually control?

I’m comfortable moving between levels — from implementation details up to system behavior — because most problems show up at the boundaries between them.

And when something works, I don’t leave it alone. I want to know why it works, and what would make it stop.

Built to be understood.
